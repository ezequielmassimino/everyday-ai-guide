Understanding AI: Myths & Data Compliance

Before diving into how AI can revolutionize your work, it's important to address common misconceptions and understand the guidelines for using AI responsibly, especially concerning data.

AI Myths: Debunked

Reality: ChatGPT is not permitted for use using a Workday email account or device. We have procured Gemini for Business which is Workday’s own instance of Gemini and the approved tool for use.

Reality: As consultants we work in VCR where Gemini is inaccessible but that doesn’t negate AI’s value. Other ways to reap benefits from Gemini exist:

  • Documentation: Much of our project documentation sits outside of VCR and we can use AI to help summarize and analyze documentation such as design documents and SOWs.
  • Research: Showing up at customer meetings is essential and AI can help provide background information on your customer, their industry, business requirements and common technology patterns.

Reality: AI’s effectiveness hinges on how it’s used. Suboptimal results may stem from:

  • Unclear Prompts: AI thrives on specificity. Vague queries lead to vague outputs.
  • Limited Context: Providing relevant context enhances AI’s understanding.
  • Lack of Iteration: AI is a tool, not magic. Refine prompts and provide feedback.
  • Ignoring Domain-Specific Tools: Gemini AI is powerful, but niche tools may exist.
Remember, AI is an assistant, not a replacement. Human expertise remains crucial.

Reality: Our Data Compliance team has guidelines. Customer Data, Professional Services Data, and Sensitive Information CANNOT be stored in Google Drive and CANNOT be used with Gemini AI. Data that is not Customer Data, Professional Services Data, or Sensitive Information can be stored in Google Drive and used with Gemini AI.

Data Compliance: Key Definitions

It's critical to understand what constitutes Customer Data, Professional Services Data, and Sensitive Information to use AI tools responsibly. Always refer to the official Information Classification Policy for the latest definitions.

Key Takeaway: As long as the data is NOT Sensitive Information AND is NOT intended to be entered into a Workday tenant, it can generally be stored in Google Drive and used with Gemini AI. Always exercise caution and consult policy.

Engineering a Good Gemini Prompt

Prompts are the steering wheel of generative AI. Well-crafted prompts act as detailed instructions, outlining the task, context, desired format, and even tone or style. The quality of your prompt directly influences the quality of the AI’s output.

You can think of Gemini for Workspace as your AI-powered assistant that can help you:

  • Improve your writing
  • Organize data
  • Create original images
  • Summarize information and surface insights
  • Research unfamiliar topics

Main considerations for an effective prompt:

Role:

Defining the role the AI should adopt (e.g., Services Executive, Engagement Manager). This helps tailor responses to specific contexts.

Task:

Clearly specifying the task or instruction for the AI to perform (e.g., summarize an article, generate creative text).

Requirements:

Providing specific requirements or constraints for the output (e.g., length, format, style, tone).

Instructions:

Offering additional instructions or guidelines (e.g., examples, preferred phrasing, specific points to address).
